## What Are Hyperparameters?

Hyperparameters are settings you choose before training begins — they control how training happens, not what the model learns. Unlike weights (which are learned during training), hyperparameters are set by you.

  question="You're fine-tuning with batch_size=4 and gradient_accumulation_steps=4. What is the effective batch size? If you change to batch_size=2 and gradient_accumulation_steps=8, does the effective batch size change?"
  answer="Effective batch size = batch_size × gradient_accumulation_steps = 4 × 4 = 16. With batch_size=2 and accumulation=8: effective = 2 × 8 = 16. Same effective batch size! The weight update is identical in both cases — gradients from 16 examples are accumulated and applied together. The only difference is memory usage (batch_size=4 uses more VRAM per step) and speed (batch_size=2 does more total forward passes, but each is smaller)."
