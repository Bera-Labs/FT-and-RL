## Why QLoRA Matters

QLoRA (Quantized LoRA, Dettmers et al., 2023) was a breakthrough paper. Before it, fine-tuning a 65B parameter model required 780 GB of GPU memory — impossible for almost anyone. After QLoRA, it became possible on a single 48 GB GPU. A 7B model now fine-tunes on a GPU with just 10–12 GB of VRAM — accessible on consumer hardware.

<ConceptCheck
  question="During QLoRA training, the base model weights are stored in 4-bit NF4 format. But when computing the forward pass, they're temporarily dequantized to float16. Why is this design better than computing the forward pass directly in 4-bit arithmetic?"
  answer="4-bit arithmetic has very limited precision — only 16 distinct values. Computing matrix multiplications in 4-bit would introduce significant rounding errors that compound through each of the model's 32+ layers, producing much lower quality outputs and gradients. By dequantizing to float16 just for computation (then discarding the float16 values — only the 4-bit storage remains), you get full float16 precision during the actual forward and backward passes, while only paying the 4-bit storage cost. It's the best of both worlds: memory efficiency of 4-bit, computational accuracy of 16-bit."
/>
