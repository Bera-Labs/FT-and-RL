<ConceptCheck
  question="An engineer claims that for a customer service chatbot, they can skip RLHF entirely and just use SFT on their historical chat logs. Under what conditions would this be adequate, and under what conditions would RLHF still be valuable?"
  answer="SFT-only is adequate if: (1) the historical logs are high quality (5-star rated, expert agent responses), (2) the desired behavior can be fully specified in examples (consistent format, clear correct outputs), and (3) you don't need nuanced judgment calls (when to escalate, how direct to be, when to push back on the user). RLHF becomes valuable when: (1) the 'right' response is subjective or context-dependent (warmth vs. efficiency tradeoffs), (2) you want the model to express appropriate uncertainty rather than confidently guessing, (3) you need to prevent sycophancy (the model agreeing with wrong user claims), or (4) you want behaviors that are hard to demonstrate in examples — like knowing when NOT to help and when to redirect. For most production chatbots, a good SFT is 80% of the value; RLHF adds the last 20% of quality that makes the difference between 'works' and 'impressive'."
