<ConceptCheck
  question="In self-attention, why do we divide the dot product (Q·K) by the square root of the dimension d before applying softmax?"
  answer="To prevent the dot products from becoming very large when d (the vector dimension) is large. Large dot products push the softmax into regions where it becomes extremely 'peaked' — essentially a hard argmax rather than a soft distribution. This kills gradients and makes training unstable. Dividing by √d keeps the variance of the dot products constant regardless of dimension size, which makes softmax behave as a proper soft weighting."
/>

## Why This Architecture Dominates

Transformers took over not just language, but vision (ViT), audio (Whisper), protein structure (AlphaFold), and code (Codex). The reason is the architecture's combination of properties:

- **Parallelizable**: all tokens computed simultaneously → uses GPUs efficiently
- **Expressive**: any token can attend to any other → captures long-range structure
- **Scalable**: performance improves predictably with size and data → scaling laws hold
- **Transferable**: pre-trained representations work across tasks → foundation for fine-tuning

The Transformer is the engine under every large language model you'll use in Parts 2 and 3 of this course.
