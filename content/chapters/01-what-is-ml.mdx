## What is Machine Learning?

Traditional programming is like giving someone a recipe: you write exact step-by-step instructions, and the computer follows them. If an ingredient is missing, the recipe breaks.

**Machine learning flips this around.** Instead of writing the rules, you give the computer:
1. A pile of examples (the **data**)
2. A signal for what's right or wrong (the **label** or **reward**)

The computer then figures out the rules on its own.

## Why ML Instead of Traditional Programming?

Some problems are too complex to write rules for. Consider:
- **Speech recognition**: Every person speaks differently. There's no rulebook that maps every possible sound to every possible word.
- **Image classification**: What makes a photo a 'cat'? The rules would be impossibly complex.
- **Language understanding**: Human language is ambiguous, contextual, and constantly evolving.

For these problems, showing examples and letting the model find the patterns is the only practical approach.
