<ConceptCheck
  question="In Phase 4, the LLM-as-judge found 7 training examples with a pattern where the model 'acknowledged but didn't answer' questions. Rather than removing them, a colleague suggests relabeling them with correct responses. Which approach is better and why?"
  answer="Relabeling is better. Removing the 7 examples just reduces your training set; you still have the same gap in coverage. If users ask questions similar to those 7 examples in production, the model will still fail — it was never taught how to handle them. Relabeling them with correct responses teaches the model the right behavior for those question types. Now it knows: 'for this type of question, here's how to actually answer it' rather than just never having seen that pattern. Removing is a last resort when you can't determine the correct response; otherwise, fix and keep."
/>
