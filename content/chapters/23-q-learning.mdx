## Q-Learning: The Foundational Algorithm

Q-learning (Watkins, 1989) is one of the most important algorithms in RL. It directly estimates the Q-function — the value of taking action a in state s — from experience, without needing to know the environment's transition probabilities.

<ConceptCheck
  question="In the Q-learning update, why do we update toward r + γ × max Q(s', a') rather than just r?"
  answer="Because the goal is to accumulate reward over the entire future, not just the next step. r alone is the immediate reward — one step's feedback. r + γ × max Q(s', a') is the Bellman target: immediate reward PLUS the expected total future reward from the next state onward, assuming we play optimally from there. Without the future term, Q-learning would be myopic — it would only learn to maximize the next reward, ignoring whether an action leads to a great or terrible position afterward. The γ × max Q(s', a') term is what makes Q-learning think ahead."
/>
