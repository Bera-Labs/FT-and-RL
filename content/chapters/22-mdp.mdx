## The Markov Property

A process has the Markov property if: **the future depends only on the current state, not on the history of how you got there.**

Formally: `P(s_{t+1} | s_t, a_t) = P(s_{t+1} | s_0, a_0, s_1, a_1, ..., s_t, a_t)`

In plain English: knowing the current state tells you everything useful. The past provides no additional information.

The robot can't determine its location from a single observation, so the future (whether it'll reach the exit) doesn't depend only on the current observation. To better approximate a Markov state: stack the last N observations (building a 'memory' of recent turns and wall detections) — this gives the agent enough history to triangulate its position. Alternatively, add more sensors (left wall, right wall, turn counter) to make the observation closer to the full state."
