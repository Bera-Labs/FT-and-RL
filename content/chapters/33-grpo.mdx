## Why GRPO Exists

PPO-based RLHF requires four neural networks simultaneously: the policy, the reference model, the reward model, and a separate critic (value network). The critic alone doubles memory and adds significant training complexity. GRPO eliminates the critic entirely.

<ConceptCheck
  question="In GRPO, why does the advantage A_i = (r_i вҲ’ Ој_r) / Пғ_r work as a substitute for the PPO critic's advantage estimate A = r + ОіV(s') вҲ’ V(s)?"
  answer="Both measure the same thing: 'was this response/action better or worse than the baseline expectation?' In PPO, the baseline is V(s) вҖ” the critic's estimate of expected reward from this state. In GRPO, the baseline is Ој_r вҖ” the actual mean reward across G responses to the same prompt. Since all G responses share the same prompt (same state), their mean reward is an empirical estimate of V(prompt). With G=8 or more samples, this empirical estimate is stable enough to serve as a reliable baseline. The division by Пғ_r normalizes the scale so advantages are consistent across prompts with different reward magnitudes вҖ” the same role that GAE normalization plays in PPO."
/>
